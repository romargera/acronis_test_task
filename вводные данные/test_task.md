### Context

Acronis serves MSPs and IT admins who protect thousands of tenants across endpoints, Microsoft 365, and Google Workspace. We're exploring a new generation of dashboards in the Acronis platform to help MSPs run and grow their business. Your task is to propose two of them.

### Prompt

Design two new dashboards for the Acronis platform:
An operations dashboard for the MSP technician / IT admin — surfacing posture, risk, trends, and operational signal they can act on.
A business-growth dashboard for the MSP owner or practice lead — focused on growing the MSP's own business. Where their next revenue is coming from, which customers are about to churn or expand, where time and margin leak, how they compare to peers. Not Acronis's growth — theirs.

#### Deliver

Problem framing & personas For each dashboard: who it's for, the top 1–3 decisions it enables, and why it matters. One or two sentences each.
Requirements Concise PRD-style section per dashboard: goals, non-goals, primary user stories, and success metrics. AI tools are expected — show your working (see #5).
Data sources & model Sketch the data behind each dashboard at a level that makes the proposal believable — not an exhaustive schema. Cover what data is needed, where it plausibly lives, and any gaps or assumptions. A few sentences or a simple table per dashboard is enough; go deeper only where it's load-bearing for your argument.
Interactive prototype Build a clickable prototype of both dashboards — not static mocks. It should run in a browser with realistic (fake) data and include real interactions (filters, drill-downs, at least one meaningful click-through flow). Submit the HTML files directly (zip is fine) — no hosting required. Include a short README noting how to open it and what's real vs. stubbed. Use whatever modern approach lets you move fastest — we care about the thinking in the prototype, not the stack.
AI workflow note (½–1 page) How you used AI during this task: where it accelerated you, where you overrode or discarded its output, and one concrete example of a judgment call you made against its suggestion. We're evaluating your judgment, not the AI's output.
Assumptions, risks, and what you'd validate next What you took as given, the biggest risks, and the first 2–3 things you'd test with MSPs before committing to build.

#### Format & Expectations

Deliverable: Plain text / markdown files for all written sections (framing, requirements, data model, AI workflow note, assumptions) + prototype HTML files. No slide decks, no Word docs, no PDFs
Timebox: 3–4 hours. A sharp, partial answer beats an over-polished one — tell us what you'd cut vs. keep with another two hours
Audience: Imagine presenting to an engineering lead, a designer, and a GTM partner in a 30-minute review

#### Evaluation Criteria

Product thinking: clear problem framing, prioritization, tradeoffs over feature lists
Domain fit: grasp of MSP workflows (technical and commercial) and multi-tenant realities
Data literacy: realistic sources, sensible metrics, awareness of instrumentation cost
Prototype quality: clarity of design intent and whether the interactions reveal product thinking rather than visual polish.
AI fluency: thoughtful, transparent use of AI as a force multiplier — not a ghostwriter
Communication: structured, concise, decision-ready

### Interview Follow-up

In the next round you'll walk us through your proposal and defend your choices live. Per Acronis policy, the interview itself is conducted without AI assistance, so be ready to speak to every decision in your submission and prototype.
